Crested Gecko

7–10" Adult Size
15–20yr Lifespan
72–80°F Ambient Temp
60–80% Humidity
18×18×24" Min Enclosure
Omnivore Diet Type

home Enclosure & Habitat

  • Adults: Minimum 18×18×24" vertical/arboreal terrarium (taller is better)
  • Juveniles: 10 gallon or 12×12×18" — don't go too large initially
  • Substrate: Bioactive substrate (ABG mix) or coconut fiber — maintain moisture
  • Plants: Live or fake pothos, bromeliads, philodendron — climbing and hiding spots
  • Cork bark: Vertical and horizontal pieces for natural hides
💡 Crested geckos are one of the easiest reptiles to build a beautiful bioactive setup for. Live plants make maintenance easier, not harder.

wb_sunny Temperature & Lighting

  • Ambient daytime: 72–80°F — they cannot tolerate temps above 85°F
  • Nighttime: Can drop to 65°F; cooler nights are beneficial
  • No supplemental heat usually needed in typical homes
  • UVB: Not required but low-level UVB (2.0 or 5.0) improves health — 10–12 hrs/day
  • Lighting: Dim LED or T5 plant light for plant growth; crested geckos prefer dim conditions
⚠️ Summer heat can be dangerous. Have a cooling plan (AC, ice packs, fans) if ambient temps exceed 82°F for extended periods.

water_drop Humidity & Water

  • Humidity: 60–80%; allow slight dry period between mistings (wet-dry cycle)
  • Misting: Mist once or twice daily (morning and evening)
  • Water: Small shallow dish and misted leaves — they prefer to drink droplets
  • Wet-dry cycle: Let the enclosure partially dry out between mistings to prevent mold

restaurant Diet & Feeding

  • Crested Gecko Meal Replacement Powder (CGD): Pangea, Repashy, or Leapin Leachie — can be a complete diet
  • Feeding schedule: Fresh CGD every 2 days; remove uneaten after 24–36 hours
  • Insects: Crickets, dubia roaches, BSFL 1–2×/week as enrichment — not required
  • Fruits: Banana, mango, papaya — offered sparingly as treats
  • Supplements: Calcium without D3 dusted on insects; not needed if feeding complete CGD

handshake Handling & Temperament

  • Generally calm and easy to handle once acclimated
  • They jump — be ready and handle over soft surfaces or low to the ground
  • Crested geckos can drop their tails (autotomy) if stressed — it will not regenerate
  • Most active at dawn and dusk (crepuscular)
  • Allow 2–4 weeks settling time before regular handling

medical_services Health & Common Issues

  • Floppy Tail Syndrome: Tail becomes limp from sleeping upside-down; add horizontal perches
  • Stuck shed: Humidity too low; mist more and add humid hide
  • Metabolic Bone Disease: From insufficient calcium/UVB; causes jaw and limb deformities
  • Cryptosporidiosis: Parasitic infection; causes wasting despite eating — requires vet diagnosis
  • Dehydration: Wrinkled, loose skin indicates dehydration — increase misting

warning New Owner Tips

  • Set up the full enclosure before bringing your animal home
  • Allow 1–2 weeks for your reptile to settle in before heavy handling
  • Find a reptile-knowledgeable vet immediately

  • Join online communities specific to this species
