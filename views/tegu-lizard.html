Tegu Lizard

Advanced Care Level
4–5ft Adult Size
15–20yr Lifespan
80–95°F Basking Temp
60–70% Humidity
8×4×4ft Min Enclosure
Omnivore Diet Type

home Enclosure & Habitat

  • Minimum size: 8×4×4 ft for adults (bigger is always better)
  • Substrate: 8–12" deep mix of coconut fiber, topsoil, and sand for burrowing
  • Hides: Multiple large hides on both warm and cool sides
  • Furniture: Sturdy branches and climbing structures; they are powerful animals
  • Security: Tegus are escape artists — use heavy-duty latches on all doors
💡 Tegus are intelligent and benefit from environmental enrichment. Rotate hides and add puzzle feeders.

wb_sunny Temperature & Lighting

  • Basking spot: 115–135°F surface temperature
  • Ambient warm side: 85–90°F
  • Cool side: 75–80°F
  • Nighttime: Can drop to 70°F
  • UVB: High-output UVB (T5 HO, 10.0 or Arcadia 12%) essential — 12–14 hours/day
  • Photo-period: Shorter days in winter trigger natural brumation
⚠️ Tegus brumate (hibernate) in winter. Reduce temps and light gradually starting in October–November.

water_drop Humidity & Water

  • Humidity: 60–80% — mist the enclosure daily or use a fogger
  • Water bowl: Large enough to soak in; change daily
  • Shedding: Increase humidity before and during shed
  • Soaking: Weekly 20-minute warm soaks support hydration and shed

restaurant Diet & Feeding

  • Juveniles: Feed daily — 50% protein (whole prey, ground turkey, eggs), 50% fruits/veggies
  • Adults: Feed every 2–3 days to prevent obesity
  • Prey items: Whole prey mice/rats, chicken, eggs, organ meats
  • Produce: Squash, leafy greens, berries, mango
  • Avoid: High-fat diets in adults; fireflies and avocado are toxic
  • Supplements: Dust with calcium + D3 2–3×/week for juveniles, weekly for adults

handshake Handling & Temperament

  • Tegus can be tamed to be very docile and even dog-like in behavior
  • Start handling sessions at 5–10 minutes and increase gradually
  • Never grab from above — approach from the side
  • Watch for tail whipping and biting in unsocialized animals
  • Daily interaction during the first year is key to a well-tempered adult
💡 Tegus recognize their owners and can learn their name. Many keepers describe them as the "dogs of the reptile world."

medical_services Health & Common Issues

  • Metabolic Bone Disease (MBD): Prevented with proper UVB and calcium supplementation
  • Respiratory infections: Often caused by too-cold or too-humid conditions
  • Obesity: Very common in adult tegus — monitor weight and feeding frequency
  • Parasites: Wild-caught tegus often carry internal parasites; fecal test recommended
  • Brumation issues: Ensure animals are well-fed and healthy before brumate
